Why Prepare? Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperature and weather patterns. While Earth’s climate naturally changes, human activities—primarily burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial pollution have dramatically accelerated this process.

These rapid changes don't just affect ice caps; they directly impact human safety through **more frequent and intense natural disasters**. Preparation is no longer optional—it's essential for survival and resilience.

Quick Climate Facts:

  • Earth is 1.1°C warmer than in the 1800s.
  • Antarctica loses 150 billion tons of ice yearly.
  • Sea levels are rising faster than ever.

Specific Climate Risks & Disasters

Identify, understand, and plan for the hazards amplified by a changing climate.

Cyclones & Storms

Learn safe zones, evacuation procedures, and how to secure property before landfall.

Floods & Heavy Rain

Never enter floodwaters. Understand flash flood risks and water contamination.

Wildfires

Create defensible space around your home and recognize signs of heat exhaustion.

Your Resilience Plan: Essential Checklist

A preparedness plan minimizes risk and maximizes recovery. Start here.

  • Emergency Kit: 3 days of food, water, and essential meds.
  • Document File: Copies of IDs, insurance, and medical records.
  • Communication Plan: Designate an out-of-state contact and family meeting points.
  • Home Safety: Secure heavy furniture and turn off utilities if needed.
  • Power Backup: Keep devices charged and have a battery-powered radio.
